The Challenge

Built for everyone.
Losing the next generation.

PEPcell began in 2008 with a single store and an ambitious idea: make cellular accessible to every South African. It worked. By the time of the rebrand, PEPcell had grown to over 500 stores nationwide, cementing its position as South Africa's largest go-to cellular retailer.

But growth brought a new problem. A brand refresh revealed a troubling decline in relevance among younger, tech-savvy customers — the very audience who would define PEPcell's next decade. These customers weren't looking for a recognisable brand name. They were looking for the best features at the best price. And PEPcell wasn't speaking their language.

"Despite significant growth and market position, PEPcell's relevance among younger, tech-savvy South Africans had quietly eroded. They were prioritising features over brand loyalty — and PEPcell needed a new strategy to meet them where they were."

Campaign Film

The Insight

They don't want a brand.
They want what it does.

The insight was clear: younger South African consumers had fundamentally shifted how they chose their devices and retailers. Brand heritage meant less. Specs, value, and a retailer that understood their world meant everything.

The rebrand challenge wasn't just visual — it was strategic. PEPcell needed to reposition itself as a retailer that gets tech, gets youth, and gets value without compromising on either. Every touchpoint — from in-store to digital to TV — had to feel fresh, energetic, and feature-forward.

Campaign Film

Creative Direction

Familiar brand.
New conversation.

The creative direction leaned into what younger customers actually cared about: real device specs, honest value messaging, and a visual energy that felt native to digital platforms. Out went the generic retail language. In came a sharper, more confident tone that talked to customers as tech-literate equals.

The campaign extended across TV, digital, social media, and in-store — ensuring the repositioning was consistent and credible at every touchpoint, from a YouTube pre-roll to a leaflet in hand at the till.
