The Challenge

A life-threatening gap
hiding in plain sight

In South Africa, one in 26 women will face a breast cancer diagnosis in her lifetime. A reality made worse not by indifference — but by the tyranny of the everyday. Women consistently put their families first, leaving little room for self-care. Breast self-examinations were being overlooked not out of neglect, but out of time.

We needed to find a way to bring self-examination into the moments women already had — without adding another task to an already overwhelming day.

The Idea

Meet women where
they already are

The insight was simple and powerful: every woman takes a shower. Every woman dries off. That brief moment — tops off, towel in hand — is the exact moment a breast self-examination should happen.

So we turned the towel itself into the reminder. PEP's Dry & Detect Towel is a specially designed pink towel featuring a discreet label with clear, step-by-step breast self-examination instructions and a QR code directing women to a campaign microsite with more information on breast cancer prevention.

By integrating this message into women's daily routines, we turned self-checks into a regular, empowering habit.

Campaign Film

The Execution

From product
to movement

PEP partnered with the Cancer Association of South Africa (CANSA) to distribute 450,000 embroidered pink towels across the country. The campaign was never just about the object — it was about creating a cultural moment around breast health.

The strategy was fully integrated: owned social channels amplified the message, micro and nano influencers made it personal and authentic, and traditional channels — radio, television talk shows — extended the reach into households across South Africa.

Each towel came with self-exam instructions printed directly on the label and a QR code to the campaign microsite, ensuring women had everything they needed at the most relevant moment possible.

The Results

A simple towel.
Extraordinary impact.

The campaign reached over 9.15 million people. Brand mentions increased by up to 350%. Engagement rates rose by 13.2%. Positive sentiment exceeded 22.2%. But beyond numbers, the Dry & Detect Towel started a national conversation about breast health that continues today.

The work was recognised at the 2025 MMA Smarties South Africa Awards with a Gold in Purpose Driven Marketing / Social Impact Marketing, two Silvers, a Loeries shortlist, and wins at the Bookmarks and Assegai Awards.
